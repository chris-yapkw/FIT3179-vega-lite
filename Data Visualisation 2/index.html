Ecological Footprint around the World

Ecological footprint is a measure which adds up all the productive areas for which a population, person or product competes. In this visualisation, we explore each country's biocapacity. Biocapacity is the productivity of a country's ecological assets. These assets include cropland, grazing land, forest land, built up land and fishing grounds.

Ecological Footprint Produced by Region

Here, we compare each region's total ecological footprint. We can see that produces the most ecological footprint out of all the regions.

Human Development Index (HDI) and Ecological Footprint

Here, we compare if there is a correlation between Human Development Index and Ecological Footprint. Human Development Index is a measure of key dimensions in human development, this includes high life expectancy, good knowledge and a decent standard of living. From the results above, we can see that there is indeed a correlation between the two, as when the Human Development Index increases, so does the total ecological footprint.

Each Country's Biocapacity

With this chloropeth map, we can see which countries have a biocapacity deficit or reserve. A biocapacity deficit means that the country does not produce enough biological materials to be used by its people. Conversely, a biocapacity reserve means that the country produces enough biological materials for its people.
